Two Population Dust Evolution

Intro

The dust evolution is based on the two population dust method fromTil Birnstiel's two-pop-py. The full dust grain size distribution is represented as just fine and coarse grains. The fine grains have a constant and uniform size, while the coarse grains are able to grow and evolve according to local conditions.

Physical Size Limits

The coarse grains have four potential conditions for their maximum size:

  • The growth limit
  • The drift limit
  • The fragmentation limit
  • The drift-induced fragmentation limit

If the radial drift of coarse grains occurs on a similar or shorter timescale than the grains can grow and replenish, then the drift size limit can be solved as

$$a_{\rm{ drift}} = f_d \frac{2 \Sigma_{d} v_{K}}{\pi \rho_s c_{s}^2} \gamma^{-1}.$$
